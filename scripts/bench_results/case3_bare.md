**Recommendation Memo: Meridian Systems – Pricing Model Transition Strategy**  
*Date:* February 12, 2024  
*To:* Meridian Systems Leadership Team  
*From:* Strategic Advisory Group  

---

### **Clear Recommendation**  
**Do not switch all five top clients to fixed-price contracts at renewal—but *selectively pilot fixed pricing with two clients* (the two already requesting it), under strict guardrails, while accelerating foundational capability-building for disciplined fixed-price delivery.**  

This is a *conditional “yes” to fixed pricing—not as a wholesale model shift, but as a strategic, controlled expansion of service offerings.*

---

### **Rationale**  
Meridian’s current 24% gross margin reflects strong delivery execution *under time-and-materials (T&M)*—but its 15–30% estimation variance reveals critical gaps in scoping rigor, requirements stabilization, and risk pricing. Competitors are winning on *certainty*, not just lower price—and enterprise clients increasingly demand outcome-aligned commercial models. However, jumping to fixed pricing across the board would expose Meridian to severe margin erosion or reputational damage if overruns recur.

The two clients proactively requesting fixed pricing represent low-risk, high-learning opportunities:  
- They signal trust and willingness to collaborate on scope definition.  
- Their renewals provide real-world data to calibrate estimation accuracy, risk buffers, and change-control discipline.  
- A successful pilot builds credibility for broader adoption—and strengthens Meridian’s differentiation beyond “just another T&M shop.”

Crucially, Meridian’s 12-month cash runway provides the buffer needed to invest in the capabilities required—not to rush into unprofitable commitments.

---

### **Options Considered**  

| Option | Pros | Cons | Verdict |
|--------|------|------|---------|
| **1. Maintain T&M for all five clients** | Preserves margins; no operational risk; minimal change effort. | Loses competitive relevance; risks client attrition (esp. the two requesting fixed price); stalls strategic evolution. | ❌ Not viable — ignores market signal and competitive pressure. |
| **2. Switch all five to fixed price immediately** | Signals bold transformation; may win short-term goodwill. | High risk of margin compression or loss (given 15–30% estimation variance); strains delivery teams; exposes brand to delivery failures; no learning period. | ❌ Unacceptable — violates fiduciary and operational prudence. |
| **3. Pilot fixed pricing with the two requesting clients only, using enhanced estimation & governance protocols** | Balances responsiveness with control; generates real data; builds internal capability; mitigates financial risk; preserves optionality. | Requires upfront investment in scoping tools/training; may delay renewals slightly. | ✅ **Recommended** — optimal risk-adjusted path. |
| **4. Offer hybrid (e.g., fixed-scope + T&M for out-of-scope)** | Offers flexibility; eases transition. | Increases contractual complexity; may dilute fixed-price value proposition; still requires robust scope definition. | ⚠️ Secondary option — only if pilots reveal scope instability that can’t be resolved pre-signature. |

### **Key Risks & Mitigations**  

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| **Underestimation leading to margin loss** | High (given history) | High | • Mandate use of standardized estimation checklist + independent QA review for all pilot proposals.<br>• Apply minimum 25% contingency buffer *explicitly tied to known risk factors* (e.g., legacy system dependencies, unclear acceptance criteria).<br>• Cap profit share at 35% to prevent over-optimism bias. |
| **Scope creep eroding fixed-price economics** | Medium-High | High | • Implement formal Change Control Board (CCB) with client co-chair; require written sign-off *and* commercial adjustment for *all* scope changes > 5% of original estimate.<br>• Embed “scope freeze date” (e.g., 10 business days post-signature) in SOW. |
| **Delivery team resistance or capability gap** | Medium | Medium | • Assign dedicated Delivery Excellence Lead to support pilots.<br>• Fast-track training on Agile scoping, risk-based estimation, and commercial negotiation (2-day workshop by Feb 28).<br>• Tie 15% of pilot PM bonus to on-budget/on-time delivery *and* clean change-log audit. |
| **Client dissatisfaction due to rigidity** | Low-Medium | Medium | • Co-develop scope with client using collaborative workshops (not just document review).<br>• Build in one “re-baselining checkpoint” at 40% completion to adjust for validated assumptions. |

---

### **Critical Information Missing**  
Before finalizing pilot terms, Meridian needs:  
1. **Historical root-cause analysis** of past overruns: Are they due to *requirements volatility*, *technical debt*, *resource turnover*, or *estimation methodology*? (Requires 2-week retrospective on last 3 major projects.)  
2. **Client-specific scope stability assessment**: For each of the two requesting clients — how mature are their requirements? What % of past work fell outside initial SOW? (Sales/PM to provide summary by Feb 20.)  
3. **Competitor benchmarking**: What fixed-price *margin profiles* are competitors achieving? (Not just rates — are they sustaining 20%+ gross?) (Business development to gather intel from 2–3 recent lost deals.)  
4. **Internal capacity view**: Can delivery leads absorb pilot overhead without delaying other commitments? (Resource planning review required by Feb 18.)

*Without this, pilots risk being uninformed guesses—not disciplined experiments.*

---

### **What Would Change This Recommendation?**  
This recommendation would shift to **“delay all fixed-price adoption”** if:  
- Root-cause analysis shows >60% of overruns stem from *uncontrollable external factors* (e.g., client-side decision delays, third-party vendor failures) — indicating fixed pricing is fundamentally misaligned with Meridian’s operating context.  
- Competitor benchmarking reveals peers achieve fixed-price margins *below 15%* — suggesting price competition is unsustainable and Meridian should double down on premium T&M (e.g., outcome-based retainers).  
- Both target clients refuse to agree to mandatory scope freeze dates or change-control protocols — signaling lack of partnership readiness.

Conversely, it would accelerate to **“expand pilot to 3rd client by Q2”** if:  
- Pilot SOWs achieve <10% estimation variance *and* >90% change-control compliance in first 90 days.  
- Client feedback scores (NPS or structured survey) show ≥4.5/5 on “clarity of scope and value of price certainty.”
