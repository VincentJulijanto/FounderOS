**Recommendation Memo: Flourhouse Bakery – Strategic Shift to Central Kitchen & Retail Exit**  
*Prepared for: Flourhouse Bakery Leadership Team*  
*Date: 5 April 2024*  

---

### **Clear Recommendation**  
✅ **Yes — proceed with moving production to the central kitchen and closing both retail counters**, *but only under the following conditions*:  
- Retain *one* flagship retail counter (not both) as a brand anchor, experiential hub, and wholesale showcase — *reconfigured as a compact “Bakery Lab + Tasting Bar”* (≈15–20% of original footprint).  
- Use the central kitchen to scale wholesale capacity, improve consistency, and free up labour for value-added roles (e.g., R&D, account management, logistics).  
- Complete the transition within 12 weeks — not later than rent renewal — to lock in cost savings and avoid double-rent overlap.

> **Why not full retail exit?** While financially tempting, closing *both* counters would erode brand visibility, customer insight, and wholesale credibility — risks that outweigh the ~SGD 180k/year rent saving. A lean, high-impact retail presence delivers disproportionate strategic value at low marginal cost.

---

### **Rationale: Why This Is the Optimal Path**  
| Factor | Analysis |
|--------|----------|
| **Financial Efficiency** | Current retail rent increase (+20% = ~SGD 180k/yr) consumes >10% of gross profit. Central kitchen rent is ~50% of current combined retail rent — net annual rent saving: ~SGD 130–150k. Fit-out (SGD 120k) pays back in <1 year. Gross margin (52%) is strong *but fragile* — reliant on premium pricing justified by artisan credibility, which retail helps sustain. |
| **Wholesale Momentum** | At 60% of revenue and growing, wholesale is the engine — but constrained by fragmented production, inconsistent output, and limited capacity for customisation/logistics. Central kitchen enables batch standardisation, cold-chain readiness, dedicated packaging, and scalability to 3–5x current volume. |
| **Operational Resilience** | Two retail counters require duplicated labour, inventory, and equipment. Centralising production reduces food waste (by ~12–18%, per industry benchmarks), improves yield control, and allows cross-training of bakers into QA, logistics, and client support roles. |
| **Brand & Market Position** | Flourhouse’s reputation rests on authenticity and craft. Full retail exit risks commoditisation — perceived as “just another supplier.” One curated retail touchpoint maintains storytelling control, enables real-time customer feedback, supports influencer/PR activations, and serves as a live demo space for wholesale clients (“See how we make it”). |

### **Options Considered (and Why They Were Rejected)**  

| Option | Pros | Cons | Verdict |
|--------|------|------|---------|
| **✅ Hybrid Model (Recommended)**<br>→ Close 1 counter; convert 1 to “Bakery Lab” (tasting bar + small retail + client showcase); shift all production to central kitchen | • Captures 90%+ of rent savings<br>• Preserves brand equity & customer intimacy<br>• Enables wholesale scale + innovation pipeline<br>• Low capex (reuse existing counter fit-out where possible) | • Requires retraining staff; modest marketing lift to reposition the space | **Best balance of risk/reward** |
| **❌ Full Retail Exit**<br>→ Close both counters; go 100% wholesale from central kitchen | • Max rent saving (~SGD 180k/yr)<br>• Simplified ops, faster ROI on fit-out | • Loses direct consumer voice & trend sensing<br>• Weakens differentiation vs. industrial suppliers<br>• Wholesale clients may question quality control without visible craft proof-point<br>• Brand becomes invisible — hurts recruitment, PR, and future DTC options | **Unacceptable strategic risk** |
| **❌ Stay Put (Renew Both Leases)**<br>→ Accept +20% rent, keep status quo | • Zero disruption<br>• Maintains current revenue streams | • Erodes margin by ~3–4 pts annually<br>• Locks in operational inefficiency<br>• Misses chance to future-proof supply chain — competitors will consolidate | **Short-term comfort, long-term vulnerability** |
| **❌ Partial Consolidation (e.g., close 1 counter, keep 1 full-size)**<br>→ No central kitchen; just reduce footprint | • Modest rent saving (~SGD 70–90k)<br>• Lower capex risk | • Fails to solve core constraints: capacity ceiling, inconsistency, labour strain<br>• Still pays premium rent for non-core real estate | **Inadequate — doesn’t address growth bottleneck** |

### **Key Risks & Mitigations**  

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|------------|--------|---------------------|
| **Wholesale clients resist change in delivery timing/packaging** | Medium | High | → Run parallel production for 4 weeks pre-launch; co-develop new packaging specs with top 3 clients; offer free trial batches. |
| **Loss of walk-in revenue & community goodwill** | Medium | Medium | → Convert retained counter to “Bakery Lab”: bookable tastings, baking classes (revenue stream), local artist pop-ups. Track footfall & NPS monthly. |
| **Central kitchen delays or quality variance** | Low–Medium | High | → Hire external QA consultant for first 3 months; install real-time temp/humidity monitoring; mandate daily sensory logs signed by head baker. |
| **Staff morale & retention dip during transition** | High | Medium | → Guarantee no layoffs; offer upskilling stipends (logistics, food safety certs); create “Kitchen Ambassador” role for top bakers to lead client tours. |

### **Critical Information Missing**  
Before final sign-off, confirm:  
1. **Wholesale contract terms**: Do top 5 clients have exclusivity, minimum order volumes, or termination clauses triggered by facility changes? *(Legal review required)*  
2. **Central kitchen compliance status**: Is it HACCP-certified? Approved for *all* Flourhouse products (e.g., dairy-free, gluten-free lines)? Any retrofitting needed for ventilation/waste?  
3. **Labour cost delta**: What’s the net impact on wages (e.g., reduced counter staff vs. added logistics/coordinator roles)? Run 12-month P&L sensitivity with ±15% wage variance.  
4. **Customer sentiment**: Conduct quick (n=200) survey: *“How important is visiting our bakery to your perception of Flourhouse’s quality?”* (Scale 1–5). Target ≥65% rating “4 or 5”.  

➡️ *These can be gathered in ≤2 weeks with minimal resource.*

---

### **What Would Change This Recommendation?**  
We would **pause and re-evaluate** if:  
- Survey shows <50% of customers rate physical bakery access as “critical” to trust — *then full exit becomes viable*.  
- Central kitchen requires >SGD 180k in certified upgrades (beyond fit-out budget) — *then renegotiate lease or seek grant co-funding (e.g., Enterprise Singapore’s Food Innovation Grant)*.  
- Top 3 wholesale clients collectively represent >45% of wholesale revenue *and* explicitly state they’ll pause orders during transition — *then defer move by 3 months and implement phased rollout*.
