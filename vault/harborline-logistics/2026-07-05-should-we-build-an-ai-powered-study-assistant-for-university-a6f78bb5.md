## Decision
Should we build an AI-powered study assistant for university students as our next product, or focus on a smaller tool that solves one painful study problem first?

### Context
University students already use ChatGPT, Notion, Anki, Google Docs, and lecture slides, but these tools are disconnected. The opportunity is to create a focused AI study product that converts messy lecture material into usable study outputs.

The board needs to decide whether building a complete platform now is too broad, expensive, and difficult to differentiate, or whether a smaller MVP can prove demand faster. The decision should consider student adoption, willingness to pay, AI costs, university privacy concerns, product differentiation, technical complexity, and whether the first version can create strong retention.
