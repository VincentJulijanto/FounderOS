## Decision
Should we add temperature-controlled (cold-chain) capability to move into pharma and specialty-food logistics?

### Context
Two prospective pharma clients need cold-chain and are willing to sign if we can serve it. It is a higher-margin segment than our current electronics freight, but it requires certified equipment and staff training.

## Recommendation: proceed (confidence: medium)
Proceed — but phase it. Cold-chain is a genuine margin step-up and the demand is contracted-adjacent, not speculative. Start with a certified 3PL cold-chain partner for the first pharma contract rather than buying reefer assets, mirroring what worked on the Vietnam lane. Buy equipment only once a second pharma client is signed.

### What would change this call
If certification lead times push past the first client's go-live date, or if only one pharma client materialises, drop back to conditional and stay partnered rather than investing in owned reefer capacity.

### Dissent on record
- **capability**: We have no in-house cold-chain or pharma-compliance experience; the certification and GDP (good distribution practice) burden may be heavier than the team expects.

### Missing inputs
- Certification (GDP) lead time and cost
- Whether the first pharma client's volume alone covers the partnered cold-chain premium
- Training requirement and timeline for ops staff
