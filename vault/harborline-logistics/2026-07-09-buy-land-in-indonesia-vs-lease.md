## Decision
where should we buy land in indonesia

### Context
Need the land and space that is easily accessible through docks and be able to move the cargo into a yard, doesnt need to be really close to the docks but not too far

## Recommendation: hold (confidence: high)
The board is converging on a clear call: do not buy land in Indonesia. The operational need — dock-accessible yard and warehouse space — is real, but land acquisition conflates the need with the wrong solution for a company at this stage. The financial, legal, and organizational gaps are too large to close in two quarters. Buying land in Batam, Tanjung Priok, or anywhere else would consume a significant portion of the 16-month cash runway, expose the company to foreign ownership restrictions and permitting timelines that routinely exceed 12–24 months, and distract a small team from core operations. The board's prior decisions consistently validated asset-light approaches — the Vietnam lane pilot, the last-mile fleet hold, the disciplined bid floor — and this situation follows the same pattern: the service need is real, but ownership is not the answer yet. Lease and partnership alternatives solve the operational requirement without the capital, legal, and timeline risk.

### What would change this call
Three conditions would justify revisiting land acquisition: (1) contracted volume commitments from anchor customers in Indonesia that require dedicated, long-term yard space and cannot be served through lease or partnership arrangements; (2) secured external financing or a defined capital budget that does not erode the operating runway; (3) a realistic legal and permitting pathway with local partners that can be executed within 12 months. Until all three are in place, ownership is premature.
