## Decision
Should we raise spot-lane rates by ~8% in January, or hold pricing to protect volume while fuel and driver costs climb?

### Context
Fuel surcharges and driver wages have eaten ~3 points of gross margin over two quarters. Two regional competitors have already announced increases. Roughly 40% of revenue is spot; the rest is under annual contract and untouched by this call.

## Recommendation: proceed (confidence: medium)
Raise spot rates ~8% in January with 45 days' notice. The cost pressure is market-wide and competitors are moving first — the window where an increase reads as "the market" rather than "Harborline" is open now. Grandfather the two accounts in active annual-contract negotiations so the increase doesn't poison those conversations.

### What would change this call
If either competitor walks back its announced increase before January, drop to ~5% and re-check quarterly. If more than one mid-size account signals churn in the notice window, offer volume-tiered rebates rather than rescinding the increase.

### Dissent on record
- **growth**: An 8% increase during active contract negotiations risks the two enterprise renewals worth more than the entire spot uplift.

### Missing inputs
- Price sensitivity by account tier (past churn vs rate events)
- Competitors' effective (not announced) increases after discounts

### Learnings
- Spot and contract pricing are separate decisions; bundling them would have stalled both.
- Moving with the market beats moving alone — timing mattered more than the exact percentage.

### Outcome
**Implemented January 2026.** One small spot account (~2% of revenue) churned to a cheaper carrier; no mid-size losses. Blended gross margin recovered ~2 points by March. The grandfathering carve-out kept both enterprise renewals alive.
