## Decision
Should we buy our own last-mile delivery fleet (vans + drivers) instead of subcontracting last-mile to gig partners?

### Context
Service complaints on last-mile have risen and a competitor is marketing "our own fleet, our own SLA." We have ~18 months runway and the capex would consume roughly a third of it.

## Recommendation: hold (confidence: high)
Do not buy the fleet now. The service problem is real but it is a vendor-management and routing problem, not an ownership problem — and fixed fleet cost against seasonal, uneven last-mile volume is the classic way asset-light logistics companies lose their margin. Fix the SLA with tighter partner contracts and a routing tool first.

### What would change this call
Sustained last-mile volume density above the break-even utilisation for a fleet (roughly 70%+ van utilisation on core routes) for two consecutive quarters would justify revisiting. Until then, ownership destroys the margin that makes the business work.

### Dissent on record
- **growth**: A branded fleet is a real sales differentiator against the competitor and could win the enterprise accounts we keep losing on SLA.

### Missing inputs
- Route-level volume density and van utilisation projections
- The true cost of the current SLA failures (churn attributable to last-mile)
- Partner contracts' penalty terms — can we fix SLA contractually first?

### Learnings
- The board separated "the service is bad" from "we must own the fleet" — the second doesn't follow from the first.
- Capex against uneven volume was the decisive risk given the runway.

### Outcome
**Held — stayed asset-light and renegotiated partner SLAs with penalty clauses.** Q1 2026 gross margin held at ~31% (a fleet purchase would have pushed it into the low 20s). Service complaints down after the routing tool. The high-confidence hold was validated.
