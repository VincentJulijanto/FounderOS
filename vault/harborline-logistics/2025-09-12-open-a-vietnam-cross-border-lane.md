## Decision
Should we open a dedicated Vietnam cross-border trucking lane to serve our electronics clients moving stock out of the Ho Chi Minh corridor?

### Context
Two anchor clients have asked for it and one hinted at moving volume to a competitor if we can't. We have no assets or agents on the Vietnam side today.

## Recommendation: conditional (confidence: medium)
Open the lane, but as an asset-light partnership first — do not buy trucks or hire a Vietnam ops team until the anchor volume is contracted, not just verbal. The demand signal is real but concentrated in two customers, so the risk is building fixed cost against volume that can walk.

### What would change this call
A signed minimum-volume commitment from at least one anchor client would move this from conditional to proceed. Conversely, if neither client will commit on paper, hold.

### Dissent on record
- **skeptic**: Two-customer concentration means this is really a bet on one relationship, not a market. If the larger client churns, the lane is underwater.
- **finance**: Partnership margins will be thin (~8–10%); the return only works if volume scales past the two anchors within 9 months.

### Missing inputs
- Contracted (not verbal) volume commitments from the anchor clients
- A vetted Vietnam-side partner's rate card and reliability track record
- Customs/permit lead time for the corridor

### Learnings
- Demand was real but customer-concentrated — the board flagged this as the core risk.
- Asset-light-first was the right hedge given the concentration.

### Outcome
**Piloted Q4 2025 via a partner; volumes came in around 60% of plan.** The larger anchor delivered, the second under-shipped. Margin stayed positive but thin. Validated the decision to stay asset-light — a fleet purchase would have been underwater. Revisit scaling once a third client is on the lane.
