## Decision
Should we bid for the regional e-commerce platform's Malaysia–Singapore line-haul tender, which would add roughly 30% to our trunk volume?

### Context
The platform is consolidating from five carriers to two. Winning would be our single largest contract; losing bidders keep nothing. The tender's service terms include penalty clauses stricter than anything we run today, and incumbent pricing is rumoured to be below our current cost on the lane.

## Recommendation: conditional (confidence: medium)
Bid, but at a floor price that keeps the lane above ~12% contribution margin — walk away below it. A marquee volume anchor on the trunk route is worth pursuing, but not at pricing that turns our best lane into a loss leader with penalty exposure. Cap penalty liability at a fixed monthly ceiling in our submitted terms.

### What would change this call
If the tender's final terms make the penalty cap non-negotiable and uncapped, withdraw — the downside is asymmetric. If the platform signals a two-year term instead of one, the floor price can drop ~2 points for the volume certainty.

### Dissent on record
- **skeptic**: Bidding "disciplined" into a consolidation tender is how carriers talk themselves into winner's-curse pricing; the honest options are bid-to-win or don't bid.
- **finance**: The penalty clauses convert thin-margin volume into contingent liability; the contribution floor should be higher, not lower, than our standard lanes.

### Missing inputs
- The platform's actual volume commitments by month (tender ranges are wide)
- Incumbent carriers' renewal pricing on the lane
- Legal read on the penalty clauses' enforceability in both jurisdictions

### Learnings
- The board priced the option to walk away as part of the bid itself — the floor price is the decision.
- Concentration risk cuts both ways: the contract is a growth anchor and a dependency.

### Outcome
**Bid submitted at the floor price with the penalty cap in our terms.** Shortlisted to the final two in April; award decision pending. No pricing concessions made beyond the floor.
