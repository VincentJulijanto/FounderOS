## Decision
Should we move beyond Shopify DTC-only and get onto physical retail shelves (Watsons / Guardian) to grow reach?

### Context
Paid-social CAC has been climbing and retail feels like the next growth lever. We have never sold through a chain and don't know the slotting-fee and returns economics.

## Recommendation: conditional (confidence: medium)
Test retail with a single chain and a narrow SKU range before committing. Retail can build brand and reach, but the channel economics (slotting fees, retailer margin, returns, merchandising labour) are brutal for a small brand and can invert the unit economics that work online. Prove one chain pays before rolling out.

### What would change this call
If the pilot chain's fully-loaded channel margin (after slotting, retailer cut, and returns) stays positive for two quarters, expand. If slotting fees alone exceed the incremental gross profit, pull back to DTC.

### Dissent on record
- **finance**: Retail gross margin after slotting and retailer take will be roughly half of DTC; the reach only pays if it drives DTC repeat purchase, which is unproven.
- **skeptic**: We're chasing retail because paid social got expensive — that's running from a CAC problem, not toward a retail strategy.

### Missing inputs
- The pilot chain's slotting fee, retailer margin, and returns rate
- Whether retail presence measurably lifts DTC repeat rate (halo effect)
- Merchandising / field labour cost per store

### Learnings
- The board framed retail as an economics test, not a reach win.
- Flagged that the motivation (escaping rising CAC) was itself a signal to fix retention first.

### Outcome
**Piloted one chain (Guardian) with 3 SKUs; slotting fees and returns wiped out the channel margin.** Reach went up but the channel lost money and showed no measurable lift to DTC repeat purchase. Confirmed the finance/skeptic dissent. Pulled back to DTC and turned to fixing retention — which set up the March 2026 CRM decision.
