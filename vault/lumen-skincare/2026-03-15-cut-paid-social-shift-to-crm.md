## Decision
Should we cut paid-social spend by 40% and redirect that budget into retention (email/SMS CRM, subscription, loyalty)?

### Context
Paid-social CAC has roughly doubled in 18 months and we are buying revenue at a loss on first order, betting on repeat that we don't currently measure well. The failed retail pilot last quarter reinforced that reach isn't the problem — repeat purchase is.

## Recommendation: proceed (confidence: high)
Cut paid social and fund retention. The business's core weakness is that it acquires expensively and doesn't systematically bring customers back; a skincare brand lives or dies on repeat purchase and LTV, not on first-order volume. This directly addresses the CAC problem the retail detour tried to route around.

### What would change this call
If repeat-purchase rate does not improve within two cycles after standing up the CRM and subscription flows, the problem is product/retention-fit, not channel — and cutting acquisition further would then shrink the base too fast.

### Dissent on record
- **growth**: A 40% acquisition cut risks stalling top-of-funnel before the retention machine is proven; phase the cut rather than doing it in one step.

### Missing inputs
- Current cohort repeat-purchase rate and 6-month LTV by cohort
- Email/SMS list size and deliverability health
- Subscription attach-rate assumptions
